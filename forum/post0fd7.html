What is 2fa?

2fa, also known as 2 Factor Authentication, is a more secure way to protect your account. To put it as simply as possible, 2fa adds an extra step to the sign-in process that makes is very hard, if not near impossible for your account to be compromised. Despite what people claim, 2fa is a lot more secure than not having it. For the sake of this post, I will be referring mainly to Google Authenticator; someone who uses Authy or any other apps can chime in with info below and I can sticky or add to this post. Anyways, the way that the GA app works is that, once set up, you recieve a code that lasts for ~30-60 seconds before refreshing. To log into your account, you will need to input this code after you enter your user name and password. I believe that Authy works similarly, however I do not have the experience with it to speak factually.

What happens if the app is uninstalled/I lose my phone/etc?

Google Authenticator has this lovely feature where, using a backup code that you recieve from your Google account itself, not the app, you're able to switch your account codes to another device. It's rather simple; and once again does not require access to the app. I believe that Authy has a feature that allows you to send your codes to the cloud as well as access your codes from your PC, so that may be worth looking into if you have phone issues often or may have them in the foreseeable future. If this wasn't clear enough, or you need a tl;dr: you will not lose access to your account just because the app is uninstalled. Seemingly, Authy has their own recovery process that should prevent you from fully losing access forever.

I hope this actually helps you understand 2fa rather than just claiming that it's blockland.us and that your account is ""less secure"" or that they'll never be able to log in again because their "brother" deleted the app. That's just false. Now please, for the love of god, stop spreading misinformation.
